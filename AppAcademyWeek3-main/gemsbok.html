GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope native to arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. It is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia. The population is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

The English name "gemsbok" comes from Afrikaans, derived from Dutch. Despite similarities in facial markings, the gemsbok is not closely related to the chamois.
